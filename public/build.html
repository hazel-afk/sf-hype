Organic Popularity Certification

Complete Build Plan & Business Strategy

1 Vision

The Problem

82% of consumers don't trust online reviews. $650B is spent annually on digital advertising. Every recommendation platform makes money selling ads to the businesses they rank. AI-generated content is making it worse. Trust on the internet is broken.

The Insight

Some businesses earn their reputation. Others buy it. The difference is measurable. Apple spends $1.8B on ads while Samsung spends $14B. Tesla spends $0. In-N-Out spends $0. These companies have organic popularity that can't be bought.

The Solution

OPC (Organic Popularity Certification) is an independent authority that certifies businesses based on the ratio of organic visibility to paid promotion. Like USDA Organic certifies food, OPC certifies trust. Certification cannot be purchased or influenced.

2 Product

Classification System

Class Organic Score Meaning
Class I 90-100% Exceptional organic reputation. Near-zero ad reliance.
Class II 75-89% Strong organic presence. Primarily word-of-mouth driven.
Class III 60-74% Verified organic presence with some paid amplification.
Not Certified <60% Insufficient organic signals. Heavy paid reliance.

Certification Components

  • Official Seal: Visual badge businesses can display (like USDA Organic)
  • Certification ID: Unique identifier (OPC-2026-XXXXX) for verification
  • Source Data: Transparent methodology showing how score was calculated
  • Registry: Public database of all certified entities

3 Methodology

Data Sources

Organic Signals (Positive)
  • Reddit mentions and discussions (high weight - hard to game)
  • Twitter/X organic mentions
  • TikTok organic content
  • Google Trends search interest
  • User-generated content velocity
  • Forum discussions (HN, niche communities)
Paid Signals (Negative)
  • Platform advertising (Yelp Ads, Google Ads)
  • Meta Ad Library presence
  • Influencer sponsorship detection
  • Review velocity anomalies (fake review patterns)
  • Coordinated PR placement patterns

Scoring Formula

Organic Score = (Organic Signals / Total Visibility) × 100

Where Total Visibility = Organic Signals + Paid Signals

Signals are weighted by reliability and resistance to manipulation.

Key Principles

  1. Independence: OPC never accepts payment from rated entities
  2. Transparency: Methodology is public. Source data is viewable.
  3. Objectivity: Scores are calculated algorithmically, not editorially
  4. Auditability: Each certification includes verification trail

Unit Economics

Browser Extension (Core Growth Driver)
  • Free tier: Unlimited, shows ratings on 3 platforms
  • Premium ($9/mo): All platforms, full source data, alerts
  • Target: 5% free-to-paid conversion
  • At 1M free users → 50K paid → $5.4M ARR
Verification Badge
  • Certified businesses can embed official OPC badge on their site
  • Badge links to verification page on OPC registry
  • Class I: $499/yr, Class II: $299/yr, Class III: $99/yr
  • At 10K certified businesses → $2-3M ARR

Why Businesses Pay (Even Though Rating Is Free)

The rating is always free and cannot be influenced. But certified businesses want to:

  • Display the official badge (like SSL certificates or Michelin stars)
  • Get analytics on their organic score over time
  • Benchmark against competitors
  • Receive alerts when score changes

6 Competitive Advantage

Moats

  1. First-mover in certification: "Organic popularity" is a new category. We define it.
  2. Network effects: More users → more data → better scores → more users
  3. Trust: Unlike Yelp/Google, we don't sell ads to rated businesses. Ever.
  4. Brand: OPC becomes the standard, like USDA Organic or LEED certification

Why This Is Hard to Compete With

  • Yelp/Google can't do this - their business model depends on ad revenue from rated businesses
  • New entrants lack credibility - takes years to build trust as certification authority
  • Data moat deepens over time - historical scores become valuable
